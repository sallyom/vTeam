# New York Rangers - Team History

## Overview
The New York Rangers, often called the "Broadway Blueshirts," are one of the NHL's most storied franchises. Playing in the world's most famous arena in the world's most famous city, the Rangers combine Original Six tradition with the glitz and glamour of New York.

## Founding and Early Years (1926-1942)
- Founded in 1926 by Tex Rickard, owner of Madison Square Garden
- Named "Rangers" after Rickard's nickname "Tex's Rangers"
- Won Stanley Cup in just their second season (1928)
- Early championships in 1928, 1933, and 1940

## The 54-Year Drought (1940-1994)
After winning the 1940 Stanley Cup, the Rangers entered the longest championship drought in franchise history:
- The "1940 chant" became a taunt from rival fans
- Multiple close calls but no championships
- Franchise struggled while other Original Six teams dominated

## Notable Players
- **Mark Messier**: Captain who guaranteed and delivered 1994 Cup victory
- **Brian Leetch**: Two-time Norris Trophy winner and 1994 Conn Smythe winner
- **Rod Gilbert**: "Mr. Ranger" and all-time leading scorer until recently
- **Henrik Lundqvist**: "King Henrik," arguably the greatest Ranger goaltender
- **Mike Richter**: Stanley Cup-winning goaltender
- **Adam Graves**: Power forward and fan favorite

## Championships and Records
- **Stanley Cup Championships**: 4 (1928, 1933, 1940, 1994)
- **Conference Championships**: 2
- **Division Championships**: 8
- **Presidents' Trophy**: 3 (1992, 1994, 2015)

## Home Arenas
- **1926-1968**: Madison Square Garden III
- **1968-present**: Madison Square Garden (current version opened 1968)

## The 1994 Championship
The Rangers' 1994 Stanley Cup victory was one of the most celebrated in NHL history:
- Ended 54-year drought
- Mark Messier's guarantee in Game 6 vs. New Jersey
- Seven-game finals victory over Vancouver
- Ticker-tape parade through Manhattan

## The Henrik Lundqvist Era (2005-2020)
- Franchise goaltender who redefined the position for the Rangers
- Led team to 2014 Stanley Cup Finals
- Won Vezina Trophy in 2012
- Became the face of the franchise for 15 years
- Jersey #30 retired in 2022

## Modern Era (2000-present)
- **2012-2015**: Consistent contender, reached Stanley Cup Finals in 2014
- **2017-2021**: Rebuild featuring letter to fans
- **2022**: Eastern Conference Finals appearance
- Young core led by Artemi Panarin, Adam Fox, and Igor Shesterkin

## Notable Rivalries
- **New York Islanders**: The "Battle of New York"
- **New Jersey Devils**: Hudson River Rivalry
- **Philadelphia Flyers**: Intense divisional rivalry
- **Washington Capitals**: Modern playoff rivalry

## Madison Square Garden
- Known as "The World's Most Famous Arena"
- Unique atmosphere in the heart of Manhattan
- Celebrity fans and appearances
- Premium ticket prices reflect NYC market

## Cultural Impact
- Broadway connections with player nicknames
- Part of NYC's sporting fabric alongside Yankees and Knicks
- International fanbase due to NYC's global reach
- Featured in numerous movies and TV shows

## Team Identity
- **Colors**: Blue, red, and white
- **Logo**: Diagonal "RANGERS" text
- **Nickname**: "Broadway Blueshirts"
- **Goal Song**: Various iterations, currently "Howlin' for You"

## The Letter and Rebuild
In 2018, management sent a letter to fans announcing a rebuild:
- Transparent communication about direction
- Traded veteran players for draft picks
- Quick rebuild led to playoff success by 2020

## Current Stars
- **Igor Shesterkin**: Elite goaltender and Vezina winner
- **Artemi Panarin**: Dynamic offensive winger
- **Adam Fox**: Norris Trophy-winning defenseman
- **Chris Kreider**: Power forward and team leader

## Legacy
The Rangers represent hockey in the world's biggest market. Despite having only four Stanley Cups, their Original Six status, the 1994 championship run, and their presence in Manhattan make them one of hockey's most valuable and visible franchises. Playing at Madison Square Garden provides a unique atmosphere that makes every Rangers game feel like an event.
