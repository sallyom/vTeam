# The Stanley Cup - Complete History

## Origins and Early History
The Stanley Cup is the oldest professional sports trophy in North America, with a rich history dating back to 1893.

### Creation
- **Donated by**: Lord Stanley of Preston, Governor General of Canada
- **Year**: 1893
- **Original Cost**: 10 guineas ($48.67)
- **Original Name**: Dominion Hockey Challenge Cup
- **First Winner**: Montreal Hockey Club (1893)

### Early Era (1893-1926)
- Originally a challenge cup (any team could challenge the holder)
- Multiple leagues competed for the Cup
- Sometimes awarded multiple times per year
- Became exclusive NHL championship trophy in 1926

## Physical Trophy

### Design Evolution
- **Original Cup**: Bowl on top of base
- **Current Design**: Three-tiered trophy with removable bands
- **Height**: 35.25 inches
- **Weight**: 34.5 pounds
- **Material**: Silver and nickel alloy

### Unique Features
- Names of winning players, coaches, and staff engraved
- Bands retired when full (displayed in Hockey Hall of Fame)
- Original bowl still on top
- Travels more than any other professional sports trophy

## Notable Stanley Cup Records

### Individual Records
- **Most Cups Won (Player)**: Henri Richard - 11
- **Most Cups Won (Non-Player)**: Jean Béliveau - 17 (10 as player, 7 as executive)
- **Most Games Played in Finals**: Red Kelly - 164
- **Most Points in Finals**: Wayne Gretzky - 122
- **Most Goals in Finals**: Maurice Richard - 34

### Team Records
- **Most Consecutive Cups**: Montreal Canadiens - 5 (1956-1960)
- **Most Finals Appearances**: Montreal Canadiens - 35
- **Longest Drought Between Cups**: New York Rangers - 54 years (1940-1994)
- **Quickest Cup Win**: 1952 Detroit Red Wings - 8 games (minimum possible)

## Stanley Cup Traditions

### The Day with the Cup
- Each member of winning team gets 24 hours with Cup
- Tradition started in 1995
- Cup has traveled to over 30 countries
- Eaten from: cereal, caviar, popcorn, etc.
- Baptisms, weddings, and other celebrations

### Touching the Cup
- Players won't touch Conference Championship trophies
- Superstition about not touching Cup until you win it
- Some captains break tradition

### The Keeper of the Cup
- Phil Pritchard and other Hall of Fame representatives
- Travel with Cup at all times
- Ensure Cup's safety and dignity
- Document Cup's journey

## Stanley Cup Mishaps

### Famous Incidents
- **1905**: Ottawa Silver Seven drop-kicked into Rideau Canal
- **1924**: Montreal Canadiens left it on roadside
- **1962**: Toronto Maple Leafs caught in bonfire
- **1991**: Mark Messier dented it at nightclub
- **1994**: Rangers' Ed Olczyk brought it to Kentucky Derby winner

### Repairs and Replicas
- Three versions exist: Original, Presentation, and Replica
- Presentation Cup is what teams win
- Original bowl in Hockey Hall of Fame vault
- Regularly maintained and repaired

## Legacy
The Stanley Cup represents more than a championship; it embodies hockey's history, tradition, and culture. Its unique tradition of engraving names, traveling with winners, and being accessible to fans makes it the most cherished trophy in professional sports.
