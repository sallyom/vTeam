# National Hockey League (NHL) Overview

## Introduction
The National Hockey League (NHL) is a professional ice hockey league in North America comprising 32 teams—25 in the United States and 7 in Canada. It is considered to be the premier professional ice hockey league in the world and is one of the major professional sports leagues in North America.

## History
- **Founded**: November 26, 1917, in Montreal, Quebec
- **First Season**: 1917-18 with four teams
- **Original Teams**: Montreal Canadiens, Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, Toronto Arenas
- **Expansion Era**: Major expansions in 1967, 1990s, and 2000s
- **Current Size**: 32 teams as of 2021-22 season

## League Structure

### Conferences and Divisions
The NHL is divided into two conferences, each with two divisions:

**Eastern Conference**
- Atlantic Division (8 teams)
- Metropolitan Division (8 teams)

**Western Conference**
- Central Division (8 teams)
- Pacific Division (8 teams)

### Regular Season
- 82 games per team (41 home, 41 away)
- Runs from October to April
- Points system: 2 points for win, 1 point for overtime/shootout loss, 0 points for regulation loss
- Division winners and wild cards make playoffs

### Stanley Cup Playoffs
- 16 teams qualify (8 from each conference)
- Four rounds of best-of-seven series
- Conference champions meet in Stanley Cup Finals
- Winner awarded the Stanley Cup trophy

## Game Format
- **Periods**: Three 20-minute periods
- **Overtime**: 3-on-3 sudden death (5 minutes in regular season)
- **Shootout**: If still tied after overtime in regular season
- **Playoff Overtime**: 20-minute sudden death periods until goal scored

## Key Rules
- **Icing**: Shooting puck from behind center line past goal line
- **Offside**: Attacking player enters offensive zone before puck
- **Penalties**: Minor (2 minutes), Major (5 minutes), Misconduct (10 minutes)
- **Power Play**: Team with man advantage due to opponent's penalty
- **Fighting**: Allowed but results in 5-minute major penalty

## The Stanley Cup
- Oldest professional sports trophy in North America
- First awarded in 1893
- Named after Lord Stanley of Preston
- Unique tradition: Each player on winning team gets day with Cup
- Names of winning players engraved on trophy

## Major Awards
- **Hart Trophy**: Most Valuable Player
- **Vezina Trophy**: Best Goaltender
- **Norris Trophy**: Best Defenseman
- **Calder Trophy**: Rookie of the Year
- **Art Ross Trophy**: Leading Scorer
- **Conn Smythe Trophy**: Playoff MVP

## Economic Impact
- Multi-billion dollar industry
- Average team value exceeds $1 billion
- Revenue sharing among teams
- Salary cap system since 2005
- International revenue through broadcasting rights

## International Influence
- Players from over 20 countries
- Olympics participation (intermittent)
- World Championships annually
- Growing popularity in Europe and Asia
- NHL games played internationally
