# NHL Original Six Teams Overview

The Original Six era of the National Hockey League (NHL) refers to the period from 1942 to 1967 when the league consisted of six teams. These teams formed the foundation of modern professional hockey and created many of the sport's greatest rivalries and traditions.

## The Original Six Teams

1. **Boston Bruins** (Founded 1924)
2. **Chicago Blackhawks** (Founded 1926)
3. **Detroit Red Wings** (Founded 1926)
4. **Montreal Canadiens** (Founded 1909, joined NHL 1917)
5. **New York Rangers** (Founded 1926)
6. **Toronto Maple Leafs** (Founded 1917 as Toronto Arenas)

## Historical Significance

The Original Six era was characterized by:
- Intense rivalries between teams
- Limited player movement
- Dynasty teams dominating for extended periods
- The development of hockey's fundamental playing styles
- Creation of legendary player-coach relationships

## End of the Era

The Original Six era ended in 1967 with the NHL's first expansion, doubling the league size to 12 teams. This expansion marked the beginning of the modern NHL and the sport's growth across North America.

## Legacy

The Original Six teams remain among the most valuable and popular franchises in the NHL. Their jerseys, logos, and traditions continue to be celebrated, and games between Original Six teams often carry special significance for fans and players alike.
